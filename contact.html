Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring to the initial meeting? +

For the first meeting, just bring yourself and any questions you have. If you want us to review existing policies or pensions, bringing those documents is helpful but not essential.

Do I need to have a lot of money to work with a financial advisor? +

Not at all. We work with clients at all income levels and life stages. Whether you're just starting to save or planning retirement, we can help.

Are you independent or tied to specific companies? +

We are independent advisors, which means we can recommend products from across the whole market, not just specific providers. This ensures you get the best solution for your needs.

How are you paid for your services? +

We operate on a transparent fee structure. We'll always explain our fees clearly before you commit to anything. Different services may have different fee structures, which we'll discuss during your consultation.

How long does the financial planning process take? +

It depends on your needs. Simple mortgage advice might be resolved in a few weeks, while comprehensive financial planning is an ongoing process. We'll outline timescales during your consultation.

Do you offer virtual consultations? +

Yes, we offer flexible consultation options including in-person meetings, video calls, and telephone consultations. Choose whatever works best for your schedule and preferences.

What's the difference between will writing and the other services? +

Will writing is not regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority, while our financial advisory services (mortgages, pensions, insurance, investments) are FCA regulated. Both are important aspects of comprehensive financial planning.
