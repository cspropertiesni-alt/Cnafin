Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring to the initial meeting? +

For the first meeting, just bring yourself and any questions you have. If you want us to review existing policies or pensions, bringing those documents is helpful but not essential.

Why take financial advice? +

Vanguard's Adviser Alpha study estimates that professional advice can add around 3% a year in net returns, primarily through portfolio rebalancing, tax efficiency and behavioural discipline. The reality is that you get peace of mind and confidence with a long term plan and professional guidance.

Are you independent or tied to specific companies? +

We are Independent advisers (IFA), which means we can recommend solutions from across the whole of the market, not just specific providers. This ensures you get the best solution for your needs.

How are you paid for your services? +

We operate on a transparent fee structure. We'll always explain our fees clearly before you commit to anything. Different services may have different fee structures, which we'll discuss during your consultation.

Do you offer virtual meetings? +

Yes, we offer flexible meeting options including in-person, video calls, and telephone. Choose whatever works best for your schedule and preferences.

What's the difference between will writing and the other services? +

Will writing is not regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority, while our financial advisory services (mortgages, pensions, insurance, investments) are FCA regulated. Both are important aspects of comprehensive financial planning.
